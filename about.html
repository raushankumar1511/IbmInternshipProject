About Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), also known as the Global Goals, were adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015 as a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure that all people enjoy peace and prosperity by 2030. They are 17 goals that are integrated and indivisible, balancing the three dimensions of sustainable development: the economic, social and environmental.
